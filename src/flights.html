A high-speed interplanetary journey from Earth to Jupiter's moon, Io. The spacecraft, POLARIS-IV, will depart from Washington D.C. and arrive at the colony of New Solara on Io. Spanning a distance of 4.2 Astronomical Units (628 million kilometers), the trip will take approximately five months, with the spacecraft cruising at 0.04 times the speed of light (300,000 kilometers per second). The mission requires 150 terajoules of energy, roughly equivalent to the annual power consumption of the United States for 164 Days.
